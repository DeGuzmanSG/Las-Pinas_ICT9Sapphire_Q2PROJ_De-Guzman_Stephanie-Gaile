Our Recipes!

We have a variety of different types of recipes that your family or friends will surely enjoy!

Snack

These are snacks you can have during your breaks, as a fun thing to bring at events, and it is small.

Main Courses

Bigger meals that take longer to cook, but have more flavor and complexity in them, they are very heavy (depending on the dish) and are served to more than one person, to a family.

Dessert

They are sweet treats that you usually have at the later parts of the day, usually during meirienda times.



Cheese Rolls

Crispy on the outside, gooey on the inside. This contains very creamy cheese on the inside which is guaranteed to melt in your mouth.

Adobo

Pork and chicken covered with soy sauce and vinegar, forms an amazing salty yet sweet taste! It is one of the most iconic Filipino cuisine.

Ube Cookies

They are very soft and chewy, with a very nice ube flavor that even rivals the most famous Filipino desert, Halo-Halo. The softness will send you to heaven.

Taho

The creamy and chewy texture comes from all of its ingredients, the silken tofu, the tapioca pearls, and the brown sugar syrup. All of these combined results in a warm, tasty snack for breaktimes.

Sinigang

The soup, the tamarind and the leaves making it sour, the meat to add more texture and flavor gives it all a very sour yet savory taste, it is a very memorable dish to many.

Puto

A small and fluffy desert that can have a variety of flavors, like cheese and ube. It is very similar to the mamon, but it is bite-sited and even more delicious.
